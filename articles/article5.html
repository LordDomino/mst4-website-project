The Role of Government Laws in Securing Data Privacy
By NJ Rodrigo

As companies become more data-driven, governments have stepped in to protect consumer rights and regulate data practices. Several landmark laws now define how businesses can collect, store, and share personal information.

Major data privacy laws

General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) — Enforced in the European Union, GDPR mandates transparency, user consent, and strict data handling protocols. It gives people the right to access, correct, or delete their personal data.

California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) — This U.S. state law grants California residents the right to know what data is collected about them, request its deletion, and opt out of its sale.

These laws aim to place control back in the hands of consumers while holding companies accountable for how they manage data. However, the global digital landscape is still uneven, and enforcement varies by region.

The future of data privacy

As technology evolves, so must legislation. Stronger international cooperation, updated laws, and better enforcement mechanisms are needed to address challenges like cross-border data transfers, AI decision-making, and biometric data use.
