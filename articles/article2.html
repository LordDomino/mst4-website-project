By NJ Rodrigo

In today's interconnected world, personal data is the fuel that drives many business operations. Companies collect it through cookies on websites, mobile apps, social media, online forms, loyalty programs, and even smart devices. Every digital interaction you make leaves a footprint.

Why Do Companies Collect Data?

Customer Profiling: By analyzing your preferences, browsing behavior, and purchase history, companies create detailed profiles to offer personalized services and targeted advertisements.

Business Optimization: Data helps improve products, streamline operations, and make strategic decisions based on consumer behavior trends.

AI and Machine Learning: Your data feeds intelligent systems that recommend products, predict trends, and even detect fraud.

While these activities can benefit users through personalization and innovation, they also raise concerns. Many users are unaware of the extent of data collection and how it's being used behind the scenes. This lack of transparency can lead to misuse, breaches, and erosion of trust.
