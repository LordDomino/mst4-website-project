The Internet and Beyond

Ways to Protect Your Data Privacy Online
By Joseph Callos

With the networked world that we have today, protecting one's personal information online is a necessity. All web behavior from social media status updates to online transactions leaves behind a trail of information that, if not protected, puts your identity and your finances at risk. Although online privacy is an international issue, the issues and the solutions are most relevant in the Philippines, where incidents like massive data breaches and fresh cyber cons have raised public consciousness.

The Modern World: Current trends and the Philippine situation

Across the world, the digital revolution has offered the world unprecedented convenience of life coupled with mounting threats. Cyber attacks in the form of phishing, malware, and data breaches are becoming more sophisticated and more frequent. Regulations such as the European Union's General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) are forcing organizations to adopt stronger privacy controls.

In the Philippines, issues on data privacy have hit close to home. Celebrity cases, such as the 2016 Commission on Elections (COMELEC) data breach, exposed millions of voter records, thus the call for robust protection measures. Although the Data Privacy Act of 2012 (RA 10173) creates a legal framework, challenges on enforcement and awareness persist. This reality calls for Filipinos not only to be aware of their rights but to implement practical steps in protecting their own personal information.

The broader implications

1. Harden Passwords and Activate Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) Create Strong, Distinct Passwords: Avoid using common words or personal information. Rather, come up with strong letter-number-symbol combinations. Utilize Password Managers: Software like Bitwarden or LastPass can help generate and store different, secure passwords for all of your accounts. Turn on 2FA: A second step of verification, 2FA provides an additional layer of protection so it is harder for hackers to use your accounts.

2. Be cautious against phishing attacks and internet fraud Identify Deceptive Behavior: Cybercriminals commonly impersonate known individuals to deceive people into sharing sensitive details. Verify Before Clicking: Always check the authenticity of emails and websites, where URLs begin with "HTTPS" and are from the original source. Be Updated on Latest Scams: Bookmark websites like the National Privacy Commission for new news about latest phishing methods employed to scam Filipinos. Report Suspected Scams: In case you find suspected scams, report them to local authorities or the National Privacy Commission to assist in the combat of fraud.

3. Control Social Media Privacy Settings Limit Personal Data Sharing: Avoid disclosing sensitive data like your address, phone number, or financial information online. Set Privacy Settings: Control who sees your posts and your personal information by setting your account settings. Be Cautious with Friend Requests: Only accept friend requests to prevent unauthorized access to your private data.

4. Upgrade Devices and Software Keep Systems Current Regularly: Update your operating systems and applications regularly to benefit from the latest security patches. Install Reliable Security Software: Install popular anti-malware and antivirus software to protect your systems from attacks. Download Apps Only from Official Sources: In the Philippines, where third-party applications are the standard, download applications only from official application stores to avoid malware.

5. Don't Use Public Wi-Fi and Shop Online Carelessly Avoid Public Network Sensitive Transactions: Avoid shopping or banking on the Web while using public, non-secured Wi-Fi networks. Use a Virtual Private Network (VPN): A VPN encodes your internet connection, keeping your data safe from possible eavesdroppers, particularly on public networks. Disable Auto-Connect Options: Prevent your device from automatically connecting to hidden networks, lowering the risk of unauthorized access.

6. Monitor Your Online Trail Conduct Periodic Self-Audits: Search your name online periodically to see what personal information is available publicly. Monitor Account Activity: Check login history and activity logs for suspicious attempt at unauthorized access. Close Unused Accounts: Close or deactivate unused old accounts that you do not use to reduce potential security weaknesses.

7. Use Encrypted Communication and Secure Storage Use Encrypted Messaging Apps: Messaging apps like Signal or WhatsApp offer end-to-end encryption, which secures your messages. Secure Cloud Storage: When you store personal documents online, use services with good encryption policies. Or, encrypt them yourself or place them in password-protected archives for added security.

8. Employ Privacy-Oriented Tools Private Browsers: Utilize browsers like Brave or Firefox, which have tools to disable trackers and enhance privacy. Ad Blockers: Use programs like uBlock Origin to disable ads that may be malware-infected or tracking scripts. Data Erasure Services: Consider using tools like Incogni or DeleteMe to help erase your personal data from data broker databases.

Final thoughts

In a time of fast-paced innovation development in the virtual world, safeguarding online data privacy is everybody's responsibility. By following the aforementioned tips, Filipinos can construct an impenetrable shield from cyber attacks.
