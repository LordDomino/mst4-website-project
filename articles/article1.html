Data Privacy: An International Issue with a Local Character in the Philippines

With our hyper-connected world, each time you log in, shop online, or socialize on the web, you create digital footprints—paths of data that define your digital presence. Data privacy is the structure that safeguards these digital traces, allowing personal data to be gathered, utilized, and stored in a moral and ethical way. Though digital footprints exist globally, its relevance is extra special in the Philippines, where legislations like the Data Privacy Act of 2012 (Republic Act No. 10173) aim to protect people's rights in a more digitalized society.

4 Ways to Protect Your Data Privacy Online

With the networked world that we have today, protecting one's personal information online is a necessity. All web behavior from social media status updates to online transactions leaves behind a trail of information that, if not protected, puts your identity and your finances at risk. Although online privacy is an international issue, the issues and the solutions are most relevant in the Philippines, where incidents like massive data breaches and fresh cyber cons have raised public consciousness.
