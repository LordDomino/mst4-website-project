The Internet and Beyond

Data Privacy Defined

With our hyper-connected world, each time you log in, shop online, or socialize on the web, you create digital footprintsâ€”paths of data that define your digital presence. Data privacy is the structure that safeguards these digital traces, allowing personal data to be gathered, utilized, and stored in a moral and ethical way.

Corporate Collection? How Companies Collect and Use Your Personal Data

In today's interconnected world, personal data is the fuel that drives many business operations. Companies collect it through cookies on websites, mobile apps, social media, online forms, loyalty programs, and even smart devices. Every digital interaction you make leaves a footprint.

The Importance of Data Privacy in Social Media Platforms

In today's digital age, social media plays a major role in how people communicate, share experiences, and stay connected. Platforms like Facebook, Instagram, Twitter(X), and TikTok have made it easy to post thoughts, photos, and personal updates with a wide audience. For many, social media is a space for self-expression, building relationships, and staying informed.

4 Ways to Protect Your Data Privacy Online

With the networked world that we have today, protecting one's personal information online is a necessity. All web behavior from social media status updates to online transactions leaves behind a trail of information that, if not protected, puts your identity and your finances at risk. Although online privacy is an international issue, the issues and the solutions are most relevant in the Philippines, where incidents like massive data breaches and fresh cyber cons have raised public consciousness.

The Role of Government Laws in Securing Data Privacy

As companies become more data-driven, governments have stepped in to protect consumer rights and regulate data practices. Several landmark laws now define how businesses can collect, store, and share personal information.
